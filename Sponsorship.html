Sponsorship

I'm going to be honest with you, this program isn't easy. It takes willpower AND faith. You can't have one without the other, believe me, I've tried.
This is where a sponsor comes in, or a person that can walk you through the process so you can get out the other end.
The best way I can describe this is by example.

Sponsor 1

My first sponsor didn't really follow protocol. He was a social worker and did his part to keep me grounded.
During our meetings he helped me discover myself through psychology and social work, I learned alot,
but I was still acting out on my bottomlines (watching porn and seeing hookers).
The problem was I didn't accept my higher power, I didn't accept that their were things outside of my control.
I continued to get emotional and catastrophize everything. Sure I found myself but I never really worked on the root causes of why I acted out,
giving in to the inevitable and accepting it for what it is.

Sponsor 2

My current sponsor was different all together. He brought the social work aspect to the field as well as the Steps.
He was the best of both worlds. I thought of him as my best friend simply because we started working together,
he had my back and has been a great role model. Behavior wise, we have worked on the steps and are on step 4.
He has supported me through it because he believes I can conquer the pain and suffering that is my past and
present and come out the other end. In all honesty here, I wish my father was like this, but we can't win every battle right?

The take away here is that we as sponsors play multiple roles because we believe in the program and you. So in order to do that we must be a
pseudo-therapist at times, best friend at others, program director, and even worst enemy.
